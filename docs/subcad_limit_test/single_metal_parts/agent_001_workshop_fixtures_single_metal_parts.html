Domain: workshop fixtures and small machine-shop accessories

Each item below is one metal part only. Product assemblies and nonmetal components are explicitly out of scope.

Full description:

Make the single metal part for the product idea 'Low-Profile V-Block Clamp Jaw'. The broader use case is: Used on a milling machine vise to hold small round stock or tubing without raising the workpiece too high above the jaws. The chosen deliverable is only the metal body implied by: Hardened rectangular vise jaw insert with machined V-groove, counterbored mounting holes, and edge chamfers. It is intentionally included in the SubCAD limit corpus because: Combines prismatic jaw geometry with angled groove faces, hole placement, symmetry, relief cuts, and chamfered working edges. The part is made from 1045 medium-carbon steel, normalized using rectangular block stock. Start from one rectangular metal block or plate. Saw oversize, face all datum sides, then machine the pockets, holes, slots, angled faces, grooves, and edge treatments into that same solid piece.

Functional features: - single rectangular metal body: Machine one body with finished envelope 80 mm x 65 mm x 55 mm. - mounting hole pattern: Drill two through holes diameter 7 mm on the length centerline at X=13 mm and X=67 mm, Y=32 mm. - functional center feature: Machine a central obround slot 30 mm long x 17 mm wide through the part, centered at X=40 mm, Y=32 mm. - top relief pocket: Mill a rectangular relief pocket 25 mm x 21 mm x 24 mm deep on the top face, centered between the mounting holes. - edge chamfers: Apply 1.0 mm x 45 degree chamfers to all top outside edges and 0.5 mm chamfers to hole and slot mouths. - centered V-groove: Cut a 90 degree V-groove along the full X length on the top face, groove mouth 32 mm wide and depth 18 mm. - machined angled reference face: Machine one top reference face at 18 degrees over the last 18 mm of length. - counterbored seat: Add a central counterbore diameter 25 mm x 6 mm deep around the center feature.
